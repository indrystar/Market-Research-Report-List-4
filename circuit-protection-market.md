<p><p>Circuit protection markets are categorized into overcurrent protection, ESD protection, and overvoltage protection. Overcurrent protection safeguards circuits from excessive current flow that could potentially damage components. ESD protection shields circuits from electrostatic discharge, preventing damage from static electricity. Overvoltage protection defends circuits against voltage spikes that could harm sensitive components. These markets are crucial for ensuring the reliability and longevity of electronic devices by mitigating potential risks and hazards associated with power fluctuations and electrical disturbances.</p></p>
