<p><p>The Complementary Metal Oxide Semiconductor (CMOS) market primarily consists of two types: PMOS (P-channel Metal Oxide Semiconductor) and NMOS (N-channel Metal Oxide Semiconductor). PMOS transistors utilize holes as charge carriers, providing high noise margins and lower power consumption in specific applications, while NMOS transistors use electrons, offering faster switching speeds and better performance in digital circuits. Together, they enable efficient circuit design, with PMOS and NMOS working complementarily in various electronic devices, ensuring optimized power usage and operational efficiency.</p></p>
