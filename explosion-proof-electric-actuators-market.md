<p><p>Explosion-proof electric actuators are categorized into two main types: rotary actuators and linear actuators. </p><p>Rotary actuators convert electrical energy into rotational motion, ideal for applications such as valve control in hazardous environments. </p><p>Linear actuators, on the other hand, convert electrical energy into linear motion, making them suitable for tasks such as opening and closing doors or controlling dampers in explosive atmospheres. </p><p>Both types of actuators are designed to prevent sparks or arcs that could potentially ignite flammable gases or dust, ensuring safety in hazardous environments.</p></p>
