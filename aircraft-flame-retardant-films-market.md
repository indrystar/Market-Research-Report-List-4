<p><p>Aircraft flame-retardant films are classified into different types based on the materials used. Polyimide (PI) films are known for their high thermal stability and chemical resistance. Polyvinyl Fluoride (PVF) films offer excellent barrier properties and durability. Polyetheretherketone (PEEK) films are valued for their exceptional mechanical strength and flame retardancy. Other types of flame-retardant films may include materials like polyethylene terephthalate (PET) or fluorinated ethylene propylene (FEP), each offering unique properties for different applications in the aircraft industry.</p></p>
