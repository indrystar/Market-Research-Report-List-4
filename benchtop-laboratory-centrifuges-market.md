<p><p>Benchtop laboratory centrifuges are categorized into two main types: high-speed and low-speed centrifuges. High-speed centrifuges are designed to rotate at a fast speed and are commonly used for separating particles based on their density. They are ideal for applications requiring quick and effective separation. On the other hand, low-speed centrifuges rotate at a slower speed and are suitable for delicate samples that are sensitive to heat or require gentle processing. Both types of centrifuges play a crucial role in various research and clinical settings.</p></p>
