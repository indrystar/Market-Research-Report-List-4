<p><p>Shock detectors are categorized based on their sensing technology. Piezoelectric sensors generate electric charge in response to force, making them ideal for rapid shock detection. Piezoresistive sensors change resistance under stress, suitable for various applications. Capacitors detect changes in capacitance due to shock events, while strain gauges measure deformation to assess shock levels. Other technologies may include micro-electromechanical systems (MEMS) and optical sensors, each offering unique advantages in specific environments, contributing to a diversified shock detector market.</p></p>
