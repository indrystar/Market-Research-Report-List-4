<p><p>The Soluble Dietary Fiber market consists of various types including Inulin, Polydextrose, Pectin, Beta-glucan, and others. Inulin is a natural dietary fiber sourced from plants like chicory roots. Polydextrose is a synthetic fiber that can be used as a low-calorie substitute for sugar and fat in food products. Pectin is a soluble fiber found in fruits like apples and citrus fruits. Beta-glucan is a type of fiber found in oats and barley that has been shown to have cholesterol-lowering effects. Other soluble fibers in the market include guar gum, psyllium, and acacia fiber.</p></p>
