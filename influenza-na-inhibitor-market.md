<p><p>Influenza NA Inhibitors are antiviral medications used to treat influenza virus infection. The three main types in the market are Zanamivir, Oseltamivir, and Peramivir. Zanamivir is administered through inhalation, Oseltamivir is taken orally, and Peramivir is given through injection. These medications work by blocking the activity of the neuraminidase enzyme on the surface of the influenza virus, preventing it from spreading and causing further infection. They are commonly used during flu outbreaks to reduce symptoms and prevent complications.</p></p>
