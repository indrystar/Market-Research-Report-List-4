<p><p>Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) systems use a low electrical current to stimulate specific areas of the brain. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) systems deliver electrical currents that oscillate at a specific frequency. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) systems provide intermittent bursts of electrical stimulation. Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) systems deliver random electrical frequencies to the brain. These different types of stimulation systems are used for various research and therapeutic purposes to modulate neural activity in the brain.</p></p>
