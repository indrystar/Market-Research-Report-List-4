<p><p>Digital PCR, also known as dPCR, is a technique used to determine and quantify the amount of DNA or RNA in a sample. It is a more precise and sensitive method compared to traditional PCR, as it can accurately quantify a single molecule of nucleic acid. <p><p>Digital PCR market includes several types such as Microreactor/Orifice Plate Digital PCR, Microfluidic Chip Digital PCR, and Droplet Digital PCR. Microreactor/Orifice Plate Digital PCR uses microreactors or orifice plates to perform high-throughput digital PCR. Microfluidic Chip Digital PCR utilizes microfluidic chips for precise and efficient PCR amplification. Droplet Digital PCR breaks PCR samples into thousands of droplets for individual amplification, allowing for highly accurate quantification. These technologies cater to different needs in digital PCR, offering unique advantages in terms of precision, throughput, and sensitivity.</p></p>
