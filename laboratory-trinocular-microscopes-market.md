<p><p>Laboratory trinocular microscopes are commonly categorized into two main types based on their design and usage: Inverted Type and Upright Type. Inverted Type microscopes are ideal for observing samples that require a larger working distance, such as cell cultures or live organisms in a Petri dish. On the other hand, Upright Type microscopes are more suitable for observing solid specimens mounted on slides. Both types offer different advantages and are commonly used in various scientific research, medical, and industrial applications.</p></p>
